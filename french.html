Coq au Vin

Red wine-braised chicken with herbs, mushrooms, and soul

About the Dish

Coq au Vin is a traditional French dish from the **Burgundy** region. It features **chicken slowly braised in red wine**, alongside garlic, pearl onions, carrots, and mushrooms — creating a deeply rich and aromatic stew that embodies rustic comfort.

Originally a peasant dish, Coq au Vin has grown into a gourmet classic, symbolizing France’s passion for turning humble ingredients into culinary elegance.

Did You Know?

  • “Coq au Vin” means “rooster in wine” — originally made with older birds for deeper flavor.
  • Burgundy red wine is the traditional choice, but variations use white wine or Champagne.
  • This dish is slow-cooked for up to 4 hours in French kitchens.

Meet Chef Amélie Rousseau

Raised in the vineyards of **Lyon**, Chef Amélie spent her early years learning recipes passed down for generations. Her cooking is delicate, nostalgic, and deeply personal. With her Coq au Vin, she brings **the warmth of a French countryside table** to your plate — every spoonful filled with love.
