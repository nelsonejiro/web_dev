Schweinshaxe

Bavarian Roast Pork Knuckle served with beer gravy and sauerkraut

About the Dish

Schweinshaxe is a traditional German dish that originated in Bavaria, known for its crispy skin and tender, juicy meat. It's a symbol of German beer culture, typically enjoyed in hearty portions during festivals like Oktoberfest.

The pork knuckle is slow roasted for hours until the skin becomes crisp and the meat falls off the bone. It's served with tangy **sauerkraut**, fluffy **potato dumplings**, and a rich, malty **beer-based gravy**.

Did You Know?

  • The word *Schweinshaxe* means "pig's knuckle" in German.
  • This dish is often paired with *German lager or dark ale*.
  • Traditionally cooked in stone ovens or open fire pits.
