About the Dish

Ofe Nsala, also known as *white soup*, is a traditional dish from the Igbo people of Nigeria. It's made with fresh catfish, yam paste, and native spices ‚Äî no palm oil, just pure flavor.

Served with hot, stretchy pounded yam, it's a soup reserved for kings, guests, and celebrations in Eastern Nigeria.

Did You Know?

  • Ofe Nsala is often prepared for nursing mothers because of its richness.
  • The yam used is pounded and added to thicken the soup naturally.
  • In Igbo culture, a hearty soup reflects love and hospitality.
